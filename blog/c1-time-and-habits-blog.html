Catherine Edwards - C1 Time and Habits

Which timebox and habits techniques did you try?

I have yet to try any of the timebox or habit tecnniques from these blog posts. I'm actually wishing I had read through this issue first from Waffle.io! I'll learn to read through all of the cards before starting on them in my next sprint.

However, I have successfully used Habitica for tracking my tasks and improving my habits! I've also been using Toggl to start building awareness of how much time I am spending and using on a task.

Even better news: Habitica and Toggl integrate with each other! So I can use Habitica to build tasks, habits and intrinsic rewards, and Toggl to timebox my effort on those tasks as well!

What did you find interesting?

I found a number of things interesting across these articles:
  • The physical impact on the brain that meditation has.
  • Building smaller habits and anchoring them to established habits increases the likelihood of those new habits being successful.
  • Creating smaller habits in the first place are more likely to be achievable than larger habits.
  • Having a growth mindset means you'll naturally improve without sabotaging your own path to growth.
  • Being forgiving and kind to yourself for being human, despite the industrial revolution!

What worked for you?

I found BJ Fogg's observation about tracking weight resonated with me incredibly well. When BJ was weighing himself every day, he was subconsciouly keeping himself on track with a feedback loop. When he stopped weighing himself, his weight shot up! Fun fact: I have experienced this EXACT predicament! This is the power of habits and feedback loops!

Lesson learned: get back to weighing myself, every day!

Which will you experiment further with?

I really like the idea behind the Pomodoro technique. I'm going to try doing that at work this week.

I also will try meditating as a daily habit. I downloaded the Headspace app which I will use to coach me this week.
