Sprint 2 Cultural Blog - part 4

What did you learn from your 'mindfulness conversation'? How did the other person find it?

I was quite explicit with not telling my husband that I was having a 'mindful conversation' with him. I gave him all of the time he needed to talk about his motorbike that he had bought recently. I asked him about how it was working for him, how it made him feel, and repeated back to him these thoughts to close the loop.

Eventually, he did pick up that I was speaking a little more differently - "You seem rather forced right now," he said. I think he was surprised that I actually wanted to hear about how happy he was with his motorbike... And he grew suspicious! I talked him through what I had learned from this chapter of the book. Honestly, he shrugged it off saying, "OK, you do what you got to do." But deep down, I know he will be reflecting on it further :)

Which of the three suggested techniques will you use to sustain your practice?

Between the following three suggested techniques:

  • Having a buddy
  • Doing less than you can, and
  • Taking one breath a day,
I feel I will be taking (at least) one breath a day. I find these mindful breaths are best done at the end of the day, as I'm going to sleep. I breathe mindfully and reflect to myself, "That's today done, tomorrow is a new day."
