Blocked on a Simple Problem

What was the problem?

The Chessboard exercise of EloquentJavascript.net blocked me to the point that I completely lost faith in my abilities!

What problem solving techniques did you use?

I went through the following problem solving techniques:
  • Experimenting within loops
  • Looking at my results through Console.logging
  • Googling similar nested loop examples, and
  • Asking my peers and coaches for help.

How did you feel throughout the process?

I honestly felt frustrated, dismayed, and totally confused by what was being asked of me, and how to progress. I had not been struggling with my exercises until this point. I was determined to push through, but knew I couldn't do it by myself.

What did you learn?

I sought a different approach from my husband, who is an experienced software developer. I walked through the approaches and attempts I had made with this exercise, and he quietly and patiently observed and repeated back what I had done for his own understanding. While being a software developer, I also learnt that he is an excellent teacher. He did not give me the answer straight away: he encouraged me to try different approaches, and helped explain to me what the loops were doing in the code.

I learnt that ultimately I had to understand what my code was currently doing, what the desired end state is, and how I can revise the code to meet that need in the end state.

Blocked on a Simple Problem

What was the problem?

I was blocked on the Looping a Triangle exercise of chapter 2 of EloquentJavaScript.

What problem solving techniques did you use?

I tried several several ways to express the target end state using pseudocode. When I didn't achieve my desired end state, I referred to the MDN web docs to clarify my syntax. I repeated this process 8 or 10 times.

How did you feel throughout the process?

During this process, I felt relatively in control. I knew it was a matter of experimenting with syntax and learning from the results using console.logging.

What did you learn?

I learnt I had to be uncomfortable with the unknown, and to target my clarification from the docs (i.e. don't get too distracted by what else is available on the docs site!). I learnt that it was more than okay to check and recheck the docs: that's why they are there!
